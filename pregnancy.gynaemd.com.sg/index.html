What To Expect On Your Initial Visit to the Obstetrician

Congratulations on your successful pregnancy! We believe that each pregnancy is a unique experience and so we encourage you to be actively involved in making decisions about your antenatal care. This includes information on services that are available and support to help you arrive at your decision.

Meeting with the Obstetrician

During your pregnancy you will be given a series of antenatal appointments to check on your health and the health of your baby. We are committed to providing a pleasant and relaxed environment so that you will be forthcoming in asking questions and discussing your preferences with us. Quality care begins with good communication and a good obstetrician will take time to listen and to explain various treatment options.

FAQ

  • What pregnancy scans are available in your clinic?

    Aside from the routine pregnancy dating and viability ultrasound scans, our clinic offers the OSCAR test to assess the risk of certain genetic disorders or birth defects in each individual pregnancy. These tests may help couples decide if they want to proceed with diagnostic invasive tests.

    Both 3D and 4D scans are also available to provide a life-like image of your baby and a real-time moving image of the baby respectively. These scans should take about 30 minutes although it may take longer depending on your baby’s position and movement. These scans are not meant to replace the routine 2nd trimester screening scans.

    This form of ultrasound has changed the way in which we can all view your developing baby. Our patients find both 3D and 4D scanning very reassuring that their baby is developing normally.

    We also offer the Non-Invasive Prenatal Test (NIPT) which is a more sensitive and specific method of screening for Down, Edwards and Patau syndromes but they cost more. They can even screen for certain sex chromosome abnormalities, triploidy, mircodeletions, DiGeorge syndrome, Angelman syndrome, Cri-du-chat syndrome and Prader-Willi syndrome. The sex of the baby can also be determined.

  • How do I know if my obstetrician is properly qualified?

    In Singapore, every gynaecologist has to be registered with the Singapore Medical Council (SMC). The SMC keeps an up-to-date register of all gynaecologists licensed to practice and aims to ensure that they maintain the standards of care the public and the medical profession expects.

  • What are the charges?

    First consultation charges start from $150 and depending on the type of treatment, your obstetrician will explain the cost involved.

  • Can I walk in for an appointment or service?

    Yes you can but you may then have to wait. In order to be seen on time, you are strongly advised to make an appointment with our staff to ensure that a time slot is allocated for our gynaecologist to see you. Please call or email us for an appointment.
