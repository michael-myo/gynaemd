About Dr Christopher Ng

Dr Christopher Ng was educated in Singapore and the United Kingdom. His early desire to pursue a career as an Obstetrician and Gynaecologist stemmed from having two generations of prominent doctors in his family.

Dr Ng is experienced in antenatal and gynaecological ultrasound scans, prenatal screening, antenatal risk assessment and care, natural childbirth, instrumental vaginal deliveries (vacuum and forceps) and caesarean section, colposcopy, cystoscopy and management of patients with menopausal and fertility problems. Apart from general gynaecology surgery, he is accredited to perform Level 3 laparoscopic (minimally invasive) surgery and has an interest in infertility (IVF, SOIUI) and surgery for urinary incontinence and utero-vaginal prolapse.

What To Expect On Your Initial Visit to the Gynaecologist

There are a number reasons to see a gynaecologist. You may have a specific reason such as chronic menstrual pain, heavy bleeding or you need to do your annual pap smear. You may have concerns about a suspicious growth or experience difficulty in starting a family.

Planning Ahead

You may wish to prepare a list of questions to ask before seeing us so that you will not miss out anything important. Ask for the appointment on a day when you know you will not be having your period. Be sure to bring a list of all medications and supplements that you are taking.

Meeting with the Gynaecologist

Your visit with the gynaecologist should be a pleasant one and will begin much as a typical visit to your family physician. You will be asked about your medical and surgical history, medications, health problems, etc. We are committed to providing a pleasant and relaxed environment so that you will be forthcoming in asking questions and discussing your preferences with us. Quality care begins with good communication and a good gynaecologist will take time to listen and to explain the various treatment options.

FAQ

  • What are gynaecological examinations?

    The duration of your consultation and type of examinations involved depends on your age, your sexual history and whether or not you have any particular symptoms.

    If you are going to have any tests, your gynaecologist will explain them first so you don't have to be anxious. The four kinds of examination are general physical examination, breast examination, pap smear and pelvic examination.

  • How do I know if my gynaecologist is properly qualified?

    In Singapore, every gynaecologist has to be registered with the Singapore Medical Council (SMC). The SMC keeps an up-to-date register of all gynaecologists licensed to practice and aims to ensure that they maintain the standards of care the public and the medical profession expects.

  • What are the charges?

    First consultation charges start from $150 and depending on the type of treatment, your gynaecologist will explain the cost involved.

  • Can I walk in for an appointment or service?

    Yes you can but you may then have to wait. In order to be seen on time, you are strongly advised to make an appointment with our staff to ensure that a time slot is allocated for our gynaecologist to see you. Please call or email us for an appointment.
