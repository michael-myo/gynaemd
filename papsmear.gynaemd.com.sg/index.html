About Dr Christopher Ng

Dr Ng is experienced in antenatal and gynaecological ultrasound scans, prenatal screening, antenatal risk assessment and care, natural childbirth, instrumental vaginal deliveries (vacuum and forceps) and caesarean section, colposcopy, cystoscopy and management of patients with menopausal and fertility problems. FAQ

  • What is cervical cancer screening & pap smear?

    Cervical cancer screening involves routine Pap smears aimed at women with no symptoms. It is a simple painless test in which cells from the cervix are examined for any abnormalities that could lead to cancer. Fortunately, some early changes in the cervix can be seen long before cancer develops (pre-cancer stage). Once these changes are treated, cancer can be prevented.

  • Who should do a pap smear?

    It is recommended that all women who have ever had sexual intercourse should have regular Pap smears so that problems can be detected early and thus treated before they become serious.

  • What is colposcopy?

    Colposcopy is a way of looking at the cervix through a special magnifying device. This allows the doctor to find problems that cannot be seen by the eye alone. It is a relatively painless procedure in which the cervix is examined under magnification after acetic acid is applied to the cervix.

    Biopsies of abnormal areas are taken for further evaluation and treatment can be performed based on these results.

  • What is Human Papilloma Virus (HPV) Vaccination?

    There are over 100 identified types of Human Papilloma Virus (HPV), most of which are harmless and asymptomatic. HPV is spread through sexual contact targeting the genitals. There are 15 cancer-causing types which can lead to cervical cancer. The HPV Vaccination has been proven to provide protection against HPV infection and lifetime immunity against cervical cancer. It is however not a substitute for routine Pap smears for cervical cancer screening.
